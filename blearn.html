Brainster Learn Mobile App Design

Overview

Brainster is developing a mobile app based on the experience learn skills on the go. The idea is to learn by typing, swiping and browsing.

My Role

User Research
UI Design
Prototyping &
Testing

Brief

Learn lessions weekly/daily by following the principle of Micro Learning, Case Study Learning. If the user skips the lesson, they will have to wait a week to unlock all of the lessons in the case study.
Key features are tap, swipe and browse and learning by case studies.
Some other key features that the app must have are:

  • Gemification
  • Badges
  • User Modification
  • Admin Panel
  • User Information
  • User Profile
  • How many days in a row the user is logging in

The process

We based our research on the Double Diamond Model Theory.
We need a way to understand the users, understand the problems needed solving and opportunities. During the descovery, the team worked on finding out the target audiences, the competitors, product and the overall business objectives.
The Outcome from the discovery is listed below in more cathegories like:
User personas & High fidelity wireframes.

Competitive Analysis

We used this strategy so we could identify the major competitors and research their products. This was helpful for the design of the mobile app in ways that it helped us to identify the product and the user's needs and if the end product will meet them.
By researching the competitors, we could find the product trends and the information about what the competitors are doing right, where they are falling short.


Competitors

Duolingo
Solo Learn
Udemy
Khan Academy
Instragram
Snapchat

After determening the competitors, we started comparing their mobile apps and the features they offered. In this case we focused on the direct competitors like Duolingo, Solo Learn,Khan Academy& Udemy
and looked at the indirect competitors like Instargram &Snapchat.

SWOT Competitors Analysis

Bu answering the questions: "What does the competitors do well?", "What could the competitors improve?", "What opportunities open to us?" & "What are the competitors excelling at?"

We created a S(Strengths) W(Weaknesses) O(Opportunities) and T(Threats) analysis.

User Persona

Based on reading user reviews, researching the competitors features and products, we created a fictional character on our close to ideal user of our learning app.
Andrej is a 20yo student and in his free time on his way to classes he would like to learn new skills.

Conclusion & Takeaways

Working in team and having a great communication was a key feature for this project sucess.
Since developing just a mobile app was something new to me, i expected and got really good constructive feedback on the design from the rest of the team.

As a learning experience i can say i improved my communication skills, i learnt not to overdesign or use many prototyping features ( less is more).
Another learning experience was improving my technical skills in Figma and the design overall.

However, if there was more time, i would pay more attention to details, build more personas and use the feature prioritization more often.
